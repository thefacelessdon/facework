# Decision 008: Certification Launches at Scale with 5-Project Minimum

## The Question
When does "Facework Protocol Conformant" become a real, paid offering?

## Resolution
Certification launches at Scale stage entry, with a minimum of 5 completed
protocol runs as prerequisite. This is an 18–24 month horizon from first
engagement.

## Alternatives Considered
- 3 projects, late Beta: Rejected — 3 projects is thin proof for a
  certification mark. The mark needs enough examples that "conformant"
  has visible, verifiable meaning in the market.
- Defer / market-driven: Rejected — a defined threshold prevents premature
  launch AND prevents indefinite deferral. 5 is specific and achievable.

## Reasoning
Certification credibility is directly proportional to the number and
quality of certified projects that exist. LEED didn't become meaningful
until enough buildings were certified that the market could see the
difference. 5 projects is the minimum for pattern recognition —
potential clients can compare multiple conformant systems and see
the standard in action.

## Implications
- MVP and Beta: No certification work. Don't build audit processes,
  marks, or compliance tooling until Scale entry.
- Gate 3 items: Remain on TODOS but are deferred to Scale.
- Focus: MVP proves the output. Beta proves transferability.
  Scale proves the standard.

## Coherence Impact
High coherence. Prevents premature credibility claims. The certification
is earned by the ecosystem, not declared by the protocol author.
