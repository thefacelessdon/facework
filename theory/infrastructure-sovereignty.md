# Infrastructure Sovereignty

Infrastructure sovereignty means participants retain agency when systems evolve, fail, or exit.

## Core Principles

1. **Portability** - participants can move data, identity context, and operational state.
2. **Exit Integrity** - leaving the system does not destroy participant continuity.
3. **Control Clarity** - ownership and control boundaries are explicit and enforceable.
4. **Dependency Awareness** - third-party dependencies are visible with fallback plans.
5. **Community Alignment** - growth does not require source communities to surrender control.

## Design Requirements

- Define ownership contracts before implementation.
- Keep canonical source-of-truth boundaries explicit.
- Maintain machine-readable export paths where possible.
- Document dependency risk tiers and mitigation plans.
- Include sovereignty checks in release readiness criteria.

## Anti-Patterns

- Lock-in by hidden coupling.
- Undocumented vendor-critical paths.
- Exit processes that require bespoke support intervention.
- Monetization models that decouple source contribution from upside.
