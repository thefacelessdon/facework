# Face.works — Identity

- **Tenant:** Face.works
- **Track:** Agency / Studio
- **Practice:** GAMUT (runs Facework Protocol on tenant projects)
- **Audience:** Creators and cultural brands with active communities and
  strong identity — validated demand, not hypothetical
- **Stage:** MVP
- **Evidence level:** Validated (named users, observed behavior,
  engagement signals)
- **Domain:** Creator commerce, cultural brand infrastructure, agency
  delivery
- **Founder:** Harper (sole operator + practice author)

## Role of design

Design at Face.works is governance, not decoration. The TasteContract
(see `soul.md`) is the quality bar every artifact must pass. Output that
fails the contract does not ship, regardless of stakeholder pressure.

## Role of automation

Skills (see `skills/`) automate operational workflows. Strategic
judgment — engagement scope, pricing exceptions, no-go calls — stays
with the founder and does not get invoked through a runtime skill. This
is intentional: agency/automation-heavy systems legitimately keep human
judgment outside the runtime.

## Role of GAMUT

GAMUT is the Face.works delivery practice. Every engagement runs the
Facework Protocol (Phases 1–8) and produces a HandoffPackage including
this kind of bundle for the client.

GAMUT is also a proof artifact in itself: Face.works runs GAMUT on its
own infrastructure to demonstrate the practice on real ground.

## Relation to Facework Protocol

Face.works is one tenant of the Facework Protocol. The protocol is
open; GAMUT is one practice implementing it. Other practices may emerge
that also run the protocol. The protocol does not depend on GAMUT, and
GAMUT does not own the protocol — the protocol owns itself.
