## Identity

Facework Protocol is an **open standard for turning cultural signal into coherent,
ownable business systems** — with a commercial services and tooling layer on top.

**What it is:**
- An open protocol spec (theories, methodology, lifecycle phases, gate structure)
- A commercial implementation (agent skills, build services, certification)
- A coherence practice that prevents extraction by design

**What it is NOT:**
- A platform (Facework never owns or intermediates creator infrastructure)
- A consulting brand (the methodology is codified, not personality-dependent)
- A content product (the theory is foundational, not the revenue engine)

## Customer Segments

### Segment 1: Creators & Cultural Brands (MVP Primary)

Established creators and cultural brands who need their business systems
architected with sovereignty and coherence — not just built, but built *right*.

**What they get:** A fully architected, protocol-conformant system. Governance
docs, specs, architecture, prototype, handoff package. Everything needed to
hand to an engineer and build without meetings.

**Qualification criteria:**
- Existing creative practice or brand with economic activity
- Willingness to participate in the build process (not outsource-and-forget)
- Need for operational infrastructure they control

**Why they pay:** The alternative is either (a) build ad hoc and end up with
extraction patterns baked into the foundation, or (b) hire an agency that
builds a container they don't own. Facework builds conduits.

### Segment 2: Studios & Agencies (Beta — not MVP)

*Per Decision 002: Studios are Beta-only. Documented here for forward planning.*

Design and development studios who want to adopt Facework as their build
methodology for client work.

**What they get:** The methodology embedded in their practice. Licensed
tooling (agent skills) and the ability to certify their client
deliverables as protocol-conformant.

**Why they pay:** Differentiation. "We build with Facework Protocol" becomes
a market position.

**MVP handling:** If a studio approaches during MVP, log the interest for Beta.
They can hire Facework for a creator engagement (you run it), but cannot
license the methodology yet.

## Energy Source

**Who generates the value:**
- Bootstrap phase: The methodology author (intellectual work, operating capacity,
  cultural intelligence). This energy is finite and must be transferred.
- Sustaining phase: The creators and brands who adopt the protocol. Their projects
  give the standard credibility. Their cultural capital is what makes "Facework
  Protocol Conformant" mean something.

**How the system sustains them:**
- Creators own everything produced through a Facework engagement
- The protocol is open — they can run it themselves, hire someone else, or leave
- No lock-in to Facework services. The output is the product, not the relationship.
- Certification benefits the creator (their system is verified), not just the protocol

**Energy transfer plan:** The bootstrap phase (author as primary energy source)
must transition to the sustaining phase (creator adoption as primary energy)
within 12 months. If it doesn't, the protocol is extracting from its author —
a coherence violation.

## Ownership & Control

### What Facework owns:
- The Facework Protocol specification (open, not proprietary)
- The Facework brand and certification mark
- The commercial agent skills implementation (/fw-* tooling)
- The methodology documentation (open for reference, licensed for commercial use)

### What creators own:
- Everything produced through a Facework engagement
- All governance documents, specs, architecture, prototypes, code
- Their data, their relationships, their brand, their economic history
- The right to leave with all of the above at any time

### What studios own:
- Their client relationships (Facework never contacts their clients directly)
- Their adaptations of the methodology (within licensing terms)
- Their certification status (earned, not granted — can be revoked for cause)

### Conduit vs. Container test:
Facework builds **conduits**. The protocol is open. The deliverables belong
to the client. The relationship is finite (project-based) or arms-length
(licensing). At no point does Facework control the creator's infrastructure,
distribution, data, or economic current.
