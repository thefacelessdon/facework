# Prospect Qualification Playbook

How a creator/cultural brand goes from "interested" to "engagement starts."

**Operating model:** This is the most human-heavy playbook. Qualification
requires taste, judgment, and relationship — the agent can prepare materials
and research, but the decision to take on a creator is human.

## Trigger

Someone expresses interest in a Facework engagement.

## Steps

### 1. Pre-Conversation Research (Agent — 15 min)

Before the conversation, agent prepares:
- Research the creator/brand (public presence, existing products, community)
- Identify their current operational infrastructure (what platforms, tools,
  dependencies are visible)
- Draft questions tailored to their specific domain
- Flag any visible extraction patterns in their current setup

**Human reviews the brief.** The agent does the research; the human decides
what matters and what to ask about.

### 2. Initial Conversation (Human — 30-60 min)

Have an honest conversation. Listen for:
- What's the creative practice or brand?
- What operational infrastructure do they need?
- Do they have economic activity (not pre-revenue ideas)?
- Are they willing to participate (review sessions, decisions)?

### 3. Qualification + Pre-Extraction Check (Agent drafts → Human decides — 15 min)

Agent scores against qualification criteria and runs a preliminary extraction
check based on the conversation:

| Criterion | Signal | Pass/Fail |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Existing practice with economic activity | They make money from their craft | Required |
| Need for infrastructure they control | Platform-dependent or ad hoc | Required |
| Willingness to participate | Wants to be in the room | Required |
| Idea is not extractive | Their model doesn't extract from their community | Required |
| Budget alignment ($10K–$25K) | Can invest without distress | Required |

**Agent drafts the assessment. Human validates.** The agent can check the
structural criteria but can't judge cultural fit or extraction subtlety.

If any fail → decline honestly. If extraction is flagged but potentially
fixable → note it for Phase 1.

### 4. Scope & Propose (Agent drafts → Human refines — 1 hour)

**Agent:** Generate scope document from the engagement scope template,
tailored to the creator's domain. Include variant recommendation, timeline,
price, deliverables, payment terms.

**Human:** Refine for tone, accuracy, and anything the agent missed about
this specific creator's context. Send.

### 5. Agreement (Human)

If accepted → send creator agreement template, receive signed agreement
+ payment, schedule kickoff.

## Decision Points

- **IF they need only governance:** Foundation Only ($10K–$15K)
- **IF domain is outside expertise:** Disclose. The agent stack handles
  most domains; flag where human domain expertise needs supplementing.
- **IF you're mid-engagement:** Waitlist with specific date. Revised
  capacity (2-3 Full Protocol/month) means shorter waits.
- **IF they can't afford the range:** Don't discount. Offer to share
  protocol docs (open) so they can start governance on their own.
- **IF a studio approaches:** Log interest for Beta. They can hire you
  for a creator engagement now; they can't license the tooling yet.

## Ownership (Agent-Native)

| Step | Agent Does | Human Does |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Research | Prepares creator brief, identifies patterns | Reviews, decides what matters |
| Conversation | — | Relationship, judgment, taste |
| Qualification | Scores criteria, drafts extraction check | Validates, decides |
| Scope | Generates from template | Refines tone and accuracy |
| Agreement | — | Signs, receives payment |

## Energy Flow Check

One conversation, one check, one proposal. No funnel. No application form.
The agent accelerates preparation (research, scope drafting) so the human
can focus entirely on the relationship and judgment calls.

## Completion

Prospect is either engaged, declined (with reason), or waitlisted.
