# Go-to-Market Playbook

How you find creators/cultural brands. Solo operator + agent stack.

**Operating model:** The agent can research, draft, and prepare. It cannot
build relationships or have taste about who to approach. GTM is the most
human-heavy function after qualification — but the agent reduces the
operational overhead so the human spends time on people, not logistics.

## Trigger

Gate 1 complete. face.works live. GAMUT audit done. Ready for conversations.

## Steps

### 1. Proof Foundation (Agent prepares → Human publishes)

- [ ] Agent: Package GAMUT audit as a case study for face.works
- [ ] Agent: Generate protocol overview content from open docs
- [ ] Human: Review, refine voice, publish
- [ ] Agent: Prepare engagement scope template from delivery spec

### 2. Channel Strategy (Solo + Agent)

Revised capacity (2-4 engagements/month) means you need 3-5 qualified
conversations per month, not 1-2.

| Channel | Agent Role | Human Role | Priority |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Direct network | Research targets, draft outreach | Relationships, conversations | Primary |
| Referrals | Track and prompt follow-ups | Ask at closure | Primary |
| Creator communities | Monitor discussions, flag relevant threads | Contribute value, be useful | Secondary |
| face.works content | Draft from protocol docs + engagement learnings | Edit voice, publish | Secondary |
| Inbound from open protocol | Monitor, qualify | Respond to serious interest | Grows over time |

### 3. Outreach Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Human Time | Agent Time |
|----------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Research + reach out to 3-5 creators | Weekly | 1-2 hours | 1 hour prep |
| Community contribution | Weekly | 30 min | Monitoring |
| face.works content | Biweekly | 1 hour editing | 2 hours drafting |
| Pipeline management | Weekly | 15 min review | Automated tracking |

**Human time: ~4-5 hours/week.** Agent handles research, drafting, monitoring,
tracking. Human handles relationships, taste, and conversations.

### 4. Trust Transfer Path

A creator goes through 4 sequential trust gates before paying.
Each gate has a failure mode. Missing any gate stalls the pipeline.

| Gate | What Creates Trust | Failure Mode | Current Status |
|------|-------------------|--------------|----------------|
| 1. Protocol credibility | Open spec, clear theory, no lock-in claims | "Show me proof, not frameworks" | OPEN — spec exists, proof pending |
| 2. Social proof | Case study from a creator they respect | "That person isn't in my space" | BLOCKED — no completed engagements |
| 3. Operator credibility | Conversation proves domain understanding | "They don't get my world" | UNTESTED — no field conversations yet |
| 4. Economics clarity | Scope doc, deliverables, ownership terms | "$15K for documentation?" | READY — engagement spec exists |

**Gate 2 is the current bottleneck.** Until a completed engagement exists
as proof, trust transfer stalls after Gate 1. This is why the GAMUT audit
and first paid engagement are the highest-priority items.

**Gatekeeper awareness:** Creators don't decide in isolation. Their team
(engineer, manager, partner) and their economic controllers (label, investors)
influence the decision. If the creator isn't the sole decision-maker, the
conversation must address the gatekeeper's concerns too.

### 5. The Conversation (Human only)

Lead with their problem:
- "What does your operational infrastructure look like?"
- "Who built your current systems? Do you own them?"
- "If [platform] disappeared, what would you lose?"

If they have the problem → hand off to Prospect Qualification playbook.
If they don't → be useful. Share protocol docs. Don't force.

**Capacity check:** Never more than 3 active engagements simultaneously.
If pipeline is full, waitlist with specific dates.

## Decision Points

- **IF no qualified prospects after 1 month:** Agent researches adjacent
  channels. Human adjusts targeting.
- **IF 3 months no engagement (soft trigger):** Agent analyzes what
  conversations happened and where they stalled. Human talks to people
  who said no.
- **IF demand exceeds capacity:** This is Beta signal. Log studio interest.
  Consider licensing timeline acceleration.
- **IF agent-generated content doesn't carry the voice:** Human takes over
  content entirely. Agent provides structure, human provides frequency.

## Ownership (Agent-Native)

| Step | Agent Does | Human Does |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Research | Identifies targets, prepares briefs | Decides who to approach |
| Outreach prep | Drafts messages, materials | Refines voice, sends |
| Community monitoring | Tracks relevant discussions | Contributes when valuable |
| Content | Drafts from protocol + engagement data | Edits voice, approves |
| Pipeline | Maintains tracking, prompts follow-ups | Reviews, decides |
| Conversation | — | Relationship, taste, judgment |

## Energy Flow Check

GTM must not become a content treadmill. The agent handles volume
(research, drafting, monitoring) so the human's energy goes to
relationships and taste — the things that can't be automated and
shouldn't be.

**Self-extraction test:** If GTM still feels like a grind even with
agent support, the positioning is wrong. Fix the positioning, don't
add more effort.

## Completion

First paid engagement signed. Primary channel identified.
Cadence sustainable alongside 2-4 concurrent engagements.
