# Decision 001: MVP Stage Boundary

## The Question
What must be true before Facework can exit MVP and enter Beta?

## Resolution
Compound gate: MVP exits when BOTH conditions are met:
1. **Revenue:** Monthly revenue consistently hits $5K+ for 3 consecutive months
2. **Proof:** Minimum 3 completed protocol runs with documented outcomes, at least 1 with a public reference

Neither condition alone is sufficient. Revenue without proof is consulting.
Proof without revenue is a hobby.

## Alternatives Considered
- Revenue gate only: Rejected — hitting revenue without protocol proof means you're
  selling services, not proving the protocol
- Proof gate only: Rejected — completed runs without revenue sustainability means
  the commercial model isn't working

## Reasoning
The compound gate prevents the two most common traps for methodology creators:
(1) comfortable consulting that never becomes a protocol, and (2) intellectually
rigorous work that never becomes a business. Both gates must clear.

## Implications
- Unblocks: Clear milestone for when to invest in studio licensing (Beta)
- Constrains: Cannot launch licensing until MVP is proven on both dimensions
- Timeline: Realistic MVP exit is 6–12 months from first paid engagement

## Coherence Impact
Increases coherence. The compound gate ensures Facework practices what it
preaches — the protocol must produce real outcomes AND sustain its operator.
A protocol that extracts from its own author is incoherent.
