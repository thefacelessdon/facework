# Decision 014: Pricing Philosophy First, Ranges as Secondary Info

## The Question
Should face.works show price ranges, hide them, or lead with pricing
philosophy?

## Resolution
Lead with pricing philosophy ("You're paying for the architecture that
changes how you operate"). Show engagement variants with ranges as
secondary information — not hidden, not the headline. The CTA uses
"What problem are you trying to make coherent?" from the original copy.

## Alternatives Considered
- Prices as headline: Rejected — frames the work as a menu of
  deliverables. Facework prices coherence shifts, not outputs.
- Prices hidden entirely: Rejected — the protocol demands transparency.
  The canonical numbers are in the open business model. Hiding them
  from the site while publishing them in protocol docs is a contradiction.

## Reasoning
Facework is currently at MVP (services phase) but the vision is protocol.
The pricing philosophy does the filtering work the original copy intended
("you're paying for coherence, not deliverables") while the visible ranges
maintain the transparency the protocol demands. This is the coherent
answer for a services business built on protocol principles.

## Implications
- face.works engage page updated: philosophy first, ranges under variants
- CTA changed to "What problem are you trying to make coherent?"
- Consistent with both the original vision and the protocol's transparency
