# Decision 002: Creator-First Wedge

## The Question
Does the first paid engagement serve a creator directly, or a studio that serves creators?

## Resolution
Creator first. The first paid engagement is a direct creator/brand engagement.
Studio licensing comes after the output is proven.

## Alternatives Considered
- Studio first: Rejected — if a studio runs the protocol poorly before it's
  proven, the protocol's credibility takes a hit you can't recover from. You
  need to control quality on the first runs.
- Both sequenced: Rejected for MVP — adds complexity. Studio licensing is a
  Beta concern (see Decision 001).

## Reasoning
The protocol's credibility comes from real systems built for real creators.
A studio's testimony that "the methodology works" is weaker than a creator
holding a complete, ownable system. Creator-first also aligns with the energy
source analysis: creators sustain the ecosystem. Serve them first.

## Implications
- Unblocks: Focus for sales/outreach — target creators and cultural brands
- Constrains: No studio licensing work during MVP. If a studio approaches,
  the answer is "not yet — watch the proof develop"
- Dependency: Requires completing Gate 1 items (engagement scope template,
  portfolio proof, face.works live)

## Coherence Impact
High coherence. Serving the energy source first is the protocol's own principle
applied to itself. Building proof through creator outcomes is the strongest
possible foundation for everything downstream.
