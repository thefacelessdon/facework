# Decision 006: Identity Shifts From Services to Protocol at Beta Entry

## The Question
When does Facework stop being "you running the protocol" and start being
"the protocol others run"?

## Resolution
The identity shift IS the Beta stage transition. MVP is services — you run
the protocol for creators. Beta is when the first studio licenses the
methodology. Services don't stop, but they're no longer the only revenue
stream.

## Alternatives Considered
- At 5 completed runs: Rejected — volume of proof is necessary but not
  sufficient. The shift requires someone else successfully running it.
- Never / both coexist: Rejected as a framing — both DO coexist, but the
  transition has a defined moment. "Always both" doesn't answer when to
  invest in the licensing layer.

## Reasoning
The stage boundary creates a clear investment trigger: don't build licensing
infrastructure until MVP is proven. Don't keep doing only services once
MVP is proven. The identity shift is earned through proof, not declared
by aspiration.

## Implications
- MVP work: Focus entirely on direct creator engagements. Don't build
  licensing, training, or studio onboarding.
- Beta trigger: First studio license is the signal, not a date on a calendar
- Pricing: Services can be priced at full value during MVP — they're not
  "temporary" or "early access." They're the premium tier that persists.

## Coherence Impact
High coherence. Prevents premature scaling of the methodology before it's
proven. The identity shift is earned, not assumed.
