01. Core Concept: Patterns as System Behavior
Facework isn’t “dotted waves.”
Facework is structural alignment in motion.
Every primitive expresses itself as a behavior, not a shape.
Every engine expresses itself as a combination of behaviors interacting.
This keeps everything modular, living, and endlessly generative.
02. Base Module: The Node Line
All patterns use a base “node line” form:
* Thin vertical or horizontal strands
* Made of evenly spaced micro-nodes (not circles, not dots — points in a grid)
* Lines bend, stretch, split, merge, or vibrate depending on the primitive
* The butterfly logo already hints at this: strands under tension forming coherence
This becomes the Facework “DNA.”
Everything is made from the same material.
Think of it as:
* digital musculature
* identity tension maps
* structural rhythm
This fits the brand perfectly: you’re visualizing the forces shaping identity.
03. Primitive Patterns (7 total)
Each primitive has a distinct behavior that is easy to animate, morph, and combine.
COHERENCE (the governing primitive)
Behavior: Lines converge into a center axis
Visual: Strands tightening into alignment
Motion: Slow magnetic pull → stable lock
Tone: Calm, inevitable, structural
This is the “resting state” everything returns to.
________________


FREQUENCY
Behavior: Oscillation at a steady rhythm
Visual: Sine-like waveforms in the node lines
Motion: Consistent pulse; stable amplitude
Tone: Identity resonance, internal signature
________________


CURRENT
Behavior: Directional flow
Visual: Lines stream in a single direction
Motion: Forward movement, no backtracking
Tone: Intent, trajectory, positioning
________________


RESONANCE
Behavior: Amplitude amplification
Visual: One strand vibrates and causes nearby strands to amplify
Motion: Echo effect; ripple outward
Tone: Influence, impact, contagion
________________


FLOW
Behavior: Adaptive morphing
Visual: Lines bend around obstacles, reroute smoothly
Motion: Fluid reconfiguration
Tone: Flexibility, learning, responsiveness
________________


STABILITY
Behavior: Fixed grid, locked nodes
Visual: A rigid, even lattice
Motion: Almost no movement
Tone: Foundation, load-bearing structure
________________


ENTROPY
Behavior: Disruption, breakage, distortion
Visual: Nodes scatter; lines fray or misalign
Motion: Chaotic flicker; tension buildup
Tone: Breakdown, friction, incoherence
________________
