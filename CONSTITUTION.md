# The Constitution of Facework

*Protocols may change. Skills may change. Artifacts may change. The Constitution
remains the governing authority of the practice.*

## Preamble

Facework exists to build coherent systems.

Its purpose is to transform ideas into structures capable of maintaining
identity, integrity, and function across time, scale, and transmission.

This Constitution establishes the enduring principles of the Facework Practice.
It defines what Facework is, what it exists to do, and the boundaries within
which the practice may evolve.

## Article I — Purpose

The purpose of Facework is to establish, preserve, and restore coherence.

Facework exists to reduce structural contradiction before complexity compounds
it. Every action taken within the practice shall contribute to the creation of
systems capable of enduring beyond their creators.

## Article II — Scope

Facework is applicable to any system capable of exhibiting coherence —
individuals, products, brands, organizations, communities, institutions,
platforms, and infrastructure. The practice is independent of industry,
technology, geography, or scale.

## Article III — Foundation

Facework is the practical application of Coherence Design. Coherence Design
derives its principles from Cultural Physics. No element of Facework shall
contradict the principles established by either.

The practice occupies one layer of a five-layer model: **Theory** (Cultural
Physics) → **Discipline** (Coherence Design) → **Practice** (Facework) →
**Implementation** (Skills and Artifacts) → **Runtime** (execution
environments). Authority flows downward only.

## Article IV — The Primitive Principle

The practice shall be governed by the Facework Primitives. The primitives
describe recurring forces. They are not phases. They are not deliverables. They
are not optional. Every coherent system exhibits these forces regardless of
implementation.

## Article V — The Protocol and Postures Principle

The Protocol defines the sequence through which the primitives are applied to
**establish** coherence. Postures are the standing operating modes through which
coherence is **maintained** after it is established. The Protocol and the
Postures may evolve as the practice matures. The primitives shall not.

## Article VI — Evidence

Every meaningful action performed through Facework shall produce persistent
evidence. Evidence shall be preserved through canonical artifacts. If knowledge
cannot survive the departure of its creator, it has not yet become part of the
system.

## Article VII — Continuity

Facework exists to preserve continuity. Continuity takes precedence over
convenience. The practice shall favor systems that remain understandable,
maintainable, and transmissible across generations of builders.

## Article VIII — Sovereignty

Facework shall seek to maximize participant agency. Ownership shall be explicit.
Dependencies shall be intentional. Exit shall remain possible. No system shall
create unnecessary dependence upon itself.

## Article IX — Coherence

Every decision shall be evaluated by its contribution to coherence. Growth
without coherence is instability. Complexity without coherence is entropy.
Optimization without coherence is fragility. Coherence is the governing
objective.

## Article X — Canonical Language

Every concept within Facework shall possess one canonical definition. One
concept. One name. One meaning. Canonical language shall not be altered without
explicit revision of the practice.

## Article XI — Traceability

Every output shall be traceable. Ideas trace to principles. Decisions trace to
evidence. Architecture traces to decisions. Implementation traces to
architecture. Artifacts preserve this lineage. Nothing shall exist without
provenance.

## Article XII — Evolution

Facework is intended to evolve. Evolution shall strengthen coherence rather than
increase novelty. No addition shall duplicate an existing concept. No revision
shall weaken the internal consistency of the practice. Every change shall
improve the transmissibility of Facework.

## Article XIII — Transmission

The ultimate measure of Facework is not completion. It is transmission. A system
is coherent when another person can inherit it, understand it, continue it, and
improve it without dependence upon its original creator. This is the final test
of the practice.

## Closing Declaration

Facework is not a collection of techniques. It is a constitutional practice. Its
purpose is not merely to create work. Its purpose is to create systems capable
of sustaining meaning, preserving agency, and carrying continuity beyond those
who first imagined them.
