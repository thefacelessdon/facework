# Coherence Design™

*The discipline of designing systems that hold their shape across time, scale,
and transmission.*

*Where [Cultural Physics](cultural-physics.md) explains **why** systems cohere or
decay, Coherence Design establishes **how** coherent systems are designed — and
[Facework](the-coherence-operating-system.md) is the practice that applies it.*

---

## I. The Discipline

Most design disciplines optimize a surface: how something looks, reads, performs,
or converts. Coherence Design optimizes a different property — whether the parts
of a system reinforce the same center, so that the whole holds its shape under
pressure.

The design question is not *"is this good?"* It is *"does this remain itself as
it grows, changes hands, and meets the world?"*

Coherence Design is prescriptive where Cultural Physics is descriptive. The
theory observes the forces. The discipline decides what to do about them before
anything is built.

## II. The Object of Study

The object of study is **coherence itself** — the measurable capacity of a system
to preserve identity while adapting over time.

Coherence is not consistency (sameness) or rigidity (refusal to change). A
coherent system changes constantly at the surface while its center holds. The
discipline studies what makes that center hold, and what makes it fracture.

## III. The Shift

Coherence Design moves the unit of work from **outputs** to **systems**.

- An output is a thing produced — a page, a feature, a campaign, a deck.
- A system is the structure that produces outputs that reinforce one another.

A designer optimizing outputs produces a portfolio of disconnected artifacts. A
designer optimizing for coherence produces a system in which every output traces
to the same governing truth. The first accumulates; the second compounds.

## IV. The Designer's Responsibility

The coherence designer is responsible for **alignment across layers** —
strategy, architecture, operations, and surface. Misalignment between any two
layers is a structural debt that complexity will eventually call in.

The responsibility is not to make each layer excellent in isolation. It is to
make them reinforce one another, so that a decision at the strategy layer is
already implied by the surface, and the surface never promises what the
infrastructure cannot deliver.

## V. Principles of Coherence Design

1. **Meaning before structure.** A system can only be as coherent as the
   language through which it understands itself.
2. **Truth before aspiration.** Design for what is true when performance falls
   away, not for the pitch.
3. **Selection is governance.** Quality is produced by what a system admits and
   rejects, not by preference.
4. **Sovereignty by design.** Ownership, dependencies, and exit are design
   decisions, not afterthoughts.
5. **Evidence over intention.** A decision is not made until it is recorded with
   its reasoning; a capability does not exist until it is declared.
6. **Transmission is the test.** A design is coherent when another builder can
   inherit it and continue without its author present.

## VI. Domains of Practice

Coherence Design applies across the domains where alignment fractures:

- **Identity** — what the system is when everything performative is removed.
- **Architecture** — the structure that carries weight.
- **Operations** — how the system behaves, repeatably, under change.
- **Narrative** — how the system transmits without explanation.
- **Ecosystem** — how multiple participants (human and agent) inhabit it without
  dilution.
- **Economics** — how value is created, owned, and sustained.
- **Governance** — how decisions are made, recorded, and revisited.

## VII. The Coherence Economy

Incoherence is expensive. It shows up as rework, relitigated decisions, handoffs
that lose context, scaling that multiplies activity faster than alignment, and
trust that erodes when the surface promises what the system cannot keep.

Coherence is the inverse. A coherent system reduces the cost of decision-making
(decisions are made once), removes the need for explanation (the system
demonstrates itself), filters misaligned collaborations automatically (the
governing truth repels what contradicts it), and preserves value across
handoffs. Coherence is not a cost center. It is the compounding asset.

This is why the discipline matters most for the cultures and creators who have
always supplied the energy and rarely owned the infrastructure: coherence is how
energy becomes ownership instead of extraction.

## VIII. The Measure

A coherent design is measured by four questions:

- Does identity survive growth?
- Does the architecture survive handoff?
- Does behavior remain consistent at scale?
- Does the system remain itself when its author is gone?

## IX. Relationship to Cultural Physics

Cultural Physics provides the laws. Coherence Design provides the practice of
designing within them. The discipline does not contradict the theory; it
operationalizes it. Frequency, Current, Flow, Resonance, and Entropy are not
metaphors in the discipline — they are design variables.

## X. Relationship to Facework

Facework is the first mature practice of Coherence Design. The discipline
defines the principles; Facework defines the Protocol, the Postures, the Skills,
and the Artifacts through which the principles are consistently applied. Other
practices may follow. The discipline remains stable beneath them.

## XI. The North Star

The aim of Coherence Design is not novelty and not growth. It is **inevitability**
— systems so aligned that their evolution feels like the only possible outcome,
and so transmissible that they outlive the people who first imagined them.
